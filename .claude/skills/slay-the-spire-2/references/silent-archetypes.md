# Silent Archetypes Guide

> The Silent is considered the STRONGEST character in Slay the Spire 2. The Sly/Discard package is overpowered, allowing you to play cards for free when discarded. Shivs and Poison are much weaker than STS1.

## Character Overview

### Unique Mechanics

**Sly (NEW):**
- Cards with Sly play automatically FOR FREE when discarded
- Discarding Sly cards triggers their effect without costing energy
- This is BROKEN — you can play your entire hand for free with the right setup
- Core mechanic of the dominant archetype

**Shivs:**
- Zero-cost attacks that deal 4 damage and Exhaust
- Blade Dance now EXHAUSTS (major nerf from STS1)
- This crippled the Shiv archetype — Accuracy no longer has enough Shivs to scale

**Poison:**
- Damage over time that ticks at end of enemy turn
- NO CATALYST in STS2 — poison doesn't double anymore
- Poison is significantly weaker without scaling
- Only viable with specific setups (Noxious Fumes, Corrosive Wave)

**Weak:**
- Reduces enemy attack damage by 25%
- Silent has great Weak access (Neutralize, Leg Sweep, Piercing Wail)
- Weak is still very strong for damage mitigation

### Starter Deck (11 cards)
```
Strike x5
Defend x4
Survivor x1 (1 cost, 8 block, discard 1)
Neutralize x1 (0 cost, 3 damage, apply 1 Weak)
```
- Survivor enables Sly synergies from turn 1!
- Neutralize is free Weak — great for hallway fights
- Better starting deck than most characters

### Starting Relic
**Ring of the Snake** — Draw 2 extra cards on turn 1

## Archetype 1: Sly/Discard (DOMINANT)

**This is BY FAR the strongest Silent archetype. Most runs should aim for this.**

### Why Sly Is Broken
1. Sly cards cost 0 energy when discarded
2. Small deck + discard enablers = play entire deck for free
3. Acrobatics draws 4, discards 1 (triggers Sly)
4. Tools of the Trade triggers Sly EVERY TURN automatically
5. Can go infinite or kill turn 1 with small deck

### Core Package

**Discard Enablers (Pick ALL of these):**
| Card | Tier | Energy | Effect |
|------|------|--------|--------|
| **Acrobatics** | S | 1 | Draw 3/4 cards, discard 1. THE KEY CARD. |
| **Prepared** | S | 0 | Draw 1/2, discard 1/2. 0-cost enabler. Upgrade is insane. |
| **Tools of the Trade** | S | 1/0 | Power: Draw 1, discard 1 at turn start. Auto-Sly every turn. |
| **Survivor** | Starter | 1 | 8/11 block, discard 1. Already in deck! |
| **Calculated Gamble** | S | 0 | Discard entire hand, draw that many. TRIGGERS ALL SLY. |
| **Dagger Throw** | B | 1 | 9/12 damage, discard 1. Damage + discard. |

**Sly Payoffs (Pick 2-4):**
| Card | Tier | Energy (if played) | Effect when Discarded |
|------|------|---------------------|----------------------|
| **Reflex** | S | 3 | Draw 2/3 cards for FREE |
| **Untouchable** | B | 2 | Gain 9/12 block for FREE |
| **Flick-Flack** | B | 1 | Deal 7/9 to ALL for FREE |
| **Ricochet** | B | 2 | Deal 3×4/4×4 damage for FREE |
| **Haze** | B | 3 | Apply 4/6 Poison to ALL for FREE |
| **Abrasive** | B | 1 | Power: Gain 1/2 Dex + 4 Thorns for FREE |

**Consistency Tools:**
| Card | Tier | Effect |
|------|------|--------|
| **Well-Laid Plans** | S | Retain 1/2 cards per turn. Hold Acrobatics for perfect turn. |
| **Pinpoint** | A | Deal 17/22, costs 1 less per Skill played. Often 0 with Acro. |
| **Tactician** | A | Unplayable. Gain 1/2 energy when discarded. |

### The Dream Turn
1. Turn starts with Tools of the Trade+ (0 cost) already played
2. Auto-draw 1, discard 1 → Sly triggers (free damage/block)
3. Play Acrobatics+ → Draw 4, discard 1 → another Sly trigger
4. Play Prepared+ → Draw 2, discard 2 → two more Sly triggers
5. Play Survivor+ → 11 block, discard 1 → another Sly trigger
6. All Sly cards played for FREE
7. Play Pinpoint → costs 0 after all those Skills → 22 damage
8. Enemy is dead, you have full block

### Building the Deck
1. **Act 1:** Take Acrobatics on sight. Take Prepared. Take damage cards.
2. **Act 2:** Find Tools of the Trade. Get Well-Laid Plans. Add Sly cards.
3. **Act 3:** Remove all Strikes/Defends. Deck should be 10-13 cards.

## Archetype 2: Poison

**Two viable playstyles: Skill-Based (consistent) and Corrosive Wave (feast or famine).**

### Understanding Poison in STS2
- Poison ticks at START of enemy turn (not end)
- If poison ≥ enemy HP, enemy dies before attacking!
- **Accelerant** is the key — makes poison tick 2/3 times per turn
- No Catalyst, but Accelerant provides similar scaling

### Poison Mechanics with Accelerant
Example: Enemy has 10 poison, you have Accelerant+
- Start of enemy turn: 10 → 9 → 8 (ticks 3 times!)
- Total damage: 10 + 9 + 8 = **27 damage**
- Poison burns off faster but does MORE damage in shorter time

**Upgrade Accelerant ASAP!** (2 ticks → 3 ticks is huge)

### Playstyle 1: Skill-Based Poison (Consistent)

**Core Cards:**
| Card | Tier | Energy | Effect | Notes |
|------|------|--------|--------|-------|
| **Accelerant** | A | 2/1 | Poison ticks +1/+2 times | THE KEY CARD. Upgrade immediately. Changes everything. |
| **Deadly Poison** | B | 1 | Apply 5/7 Poison | Simple, reliable poison application. |
| **Bubble Bubble** | B | 1 | Deal 5/7, if poisoned deal again | Requires poison already on enemy. LOTS of damage. |
| **Noxious Fumes** | A | 1 | Power: Apply 2/3 Poison ALL each turn | Set up Bubble Bubble. Upgrade for +1 poison/turn. |

**Support Cards:**
| Card | Effect | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| **Blur** | Block persists to next turn | Great for poison (you're blocking while they die) |
| **Escape Plan** | 0-cost, draw 1, if Skill gain block | Free cycle + block. Skills are your deck. |
| **Prepared** | Draw 2, discard 2 | Cycle to find Accelerant/Bubble Bubble. |
| **Shadowmeld+** | Double block gain this turn (0 cost upgraded) | Turns any block card into massive block. |
| **Bullet Time+** | Cards cost 0, can't draw more | Unload entire hand. Amazing with draw setup. |

**Strategy:**
1. Apply poison with Deadly Poison/Noxious Fumes
2. Play Accelerant → poison ticks multiple times
3. Bubble Bubble for burst damage
4. Block while enemy melts from poison
5. With Bullet Time: draw into full hand, then play everything for free

### Playstyle 2: Corrosive Wave (High Variance)

**How It Works:**
- Corrosive Wave: "Whenever you draw a card, apply poison to ALL enemies"
- Combine with massive draw (Acrobatics, Backflip, Prepared)
- Each card drawn = 3/4 poison to ALL
- Draw 10 cards = 30-40 AoE poison

**Core Cards:**
| Card | Tier | Energy | Effect | Notes |
|------|------|--------|--------|-------|
| **Corrosive Wave** | A | 2 | Sly. Draw = apply 3/4 poison ALL | The centerpiece. Upgrade for more poison. |
| **Acrobatics** | S | 1 | Draw 3/4, discard 1 | More draws = more poison. |
| **Backflip** | B | 1 | Gain 5/8 block, draw 2 | Block + draws. |
| **Speedster** | B | 1 | Power: Draw = deal 2/3 damage | Extra damage per draw. Stacks with Corrosive Wave. |

**Warning:** Very feast or famine. Sometimes you draw everything, sometimes you brick.

### Relics for Poison

| Relic | Effect | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| **Snecko Skull** | +1 Poison when applying | Great with Noxious Fumes. |
| **Twisted Funnel** | Apply 4 Poison at combat start | Sets up Bubble Bubble immediately. |
| **Block relics** | Anchor, Gorgon, etc. | Block while poison kills. |
| **Energy relics** | Happy Flower, Candelabra | Play more cards per turn. |
| **Ripple Basin** | Gain energy for playing Skills | Most poison cards are Skills! |

### Counters to Watch
- **Artifact enemies** — Must waste energy stripping Artifact before poison applies
- **Multi-phase bosses** — Poison clears between phases!
- **Entomancer** — You're not attacking much, so Daze not as bad (actually good matchup!)

### Poison Strategy Summary

**Early Game:**
1. Take Deadly Poison / Noxious Fumes for poison application
2. Take Accelerant if you see it (hard pivot to poison)
3. Block cards (Blur, Escape Plan, Prepared)

**Mid Game:**
1. Find Accelerant if you haven't (the key card)
2. Add Bubble Bubble for burst
3. Upgrade Accelerant ASAP
4. Add Bullet Time for massive turns

**Late Game:**
1. Remove Strikes/Defends (more Skills = better Escape Plan)
2. Set up: Apply poison → Accelerant → Bubble Bubble
3. With Bullet Time: Draw full hand → play everything free → enemy dies

**Skill Poison vs Corrosive Wave:**
- Skill-Based: More consistent, easier to pilot, reliable wins
- Corrosive Wave: Higher ceiling, more variance, can feel amazing or terrible

## Archetype 3: Shivs

**BUFFED in STS2 thanks to Knife Trap! Different from STS1 but very viable.**

### Why Shivs Work in STS2
- **Knife Trap** is the new core — plays ALL Shivs from exhaust pile
- Shivs exhausting is now a FEATURE, not a bug
- Accumulate Shivs all fight → Knife Trap for massive burst
- Can win runs with Knife Trap alone (even without Accuracy)

### Core Build-Around Cards

| Card | Tier | Energy | Effect | Notes |
|------|------|--------|--------|-------|
| **Knife Trap** | A | 2 | Play every Shiv from exhaust pile | THE NEW CORE. Upgrade = Shiv+ (more damage). Boss melter. |
| **Accuracy** | B | 1 | Shivs deal +4/+6 damage | Still great but less essential. Knife Trap can carry alone. |
| **Finisher** | B | 1 | Deal 6/8 damage per attack this turn | Early game payoff before you find Knife Trap. |

**Priority:** Knife Trap > Accuracy (anecdotally, more wins with Knife Trap alone than Accuracy alone)

### Shiv Generators (Pick These!)

| Card | Tier | Energy | Effect | Notes |
|------|------|--------|--------|-------|
| **Cloak and Dagger** | B | 1 | 6/9 block + 1/2 Shivs | Block + Shivs. Take early even without commitment. |
| **Hidden Daggers** | B | 0 | Discard 2, add 2/3 Shivs | 0-cost! Discards statuses. Sly synergy. Great. |
| **Up My Sleeve** | B | 2→1→0 | Add 3/4 Shivs | Blade Dance that DOESN'T exhaust! Cost goes down each play. Infinite potential. |
| **Blade Dance** | B | 1 | Add 3/4 Shivs. EXHAUST. | Exhausts now but Shivs go to exhaust for Knife Trap anyway. Still good. |
| **Infinite Blades** | C | 1 | Power: Add 1/2 Shiv at turn start | Slow but consistent. Good with long fights. |
| **Leading Strike** | C | 1 | Deal 11/14 + add 1 Shiv | Weakest generator but still generates a Shiv. |

### Supplementary Cards

| Card | Effect | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| **After Image** | Gain 1/2 block per card played | #1 support card. Shivs = many cards = lots of block. |
| **Blade of Ink** | Gain +2/+3 Strength per attack this turn | #2 support card. Shivs stack this fast. 2→4→6→8... |
| **Phantom Blades** | Shivs Retain, first Shiv +9/+12 damage | Retain is situationally useful. Damage boost is nice. |
| **Phantom Knives** | Power: Shivs hit ALL enemies | AoE Shivs. Great vs Phrog, less good vs single boss. |
| **Burst** | Next 1/2 Skills play twice | Most Shiv generators are Skills! |
| **Envenom** | Attacks deal 1/2 Poison per unblocked damage | Shivs apply lots of poison stacks. Niche but works. |

### Relics to Look For

| Relic | Effect | Why It's Good |
|-------|--------|---------------|
| **Shuriken** | +1 Strength per 3 attacks | BEST Shiv relic. Shivs = Strength = more Shiv damage. |
| **Kunai** | +1 Dexterity per 3 attacks | Block scaling. |
| **Ornamental Fan** | +4 block per 3 attacks | Passive defense while you Shiv. |
| **Charon's Ashes** | 3 AoE damage per exhaust | Shivs exhaust = free damage. |

### Shiv Strategy

**Early Game (Act 1):**
1. Take Cloak and Dagger / Hidden Daggers even without commitment (they're decent)
2. If offered Knife Trap or Accuracy → hard pivot to Shivs
3. Hold off on Up My Sleeve until committed

**Mid Game (Act 2):**
1. Find Knife Trap (priority #1)
2. Get more Shiv generators
3. After Image for defense
4. Blade of Ink if offered

**Late Game (Act 3):**
1. Accumulate Shivs all fight
2. Block while building exhaust pile
3. Unload with Knife Trap for massive burst
4. Example: 49 Shivs in exhaust = one-shot boss

### Gameplay Tips

**General Loop:**
1. Spend most turns generating Shivs and playing them
2. Focus on defense with After Image / Cloak and Dagger
3. Build up exhaust pile
4. When ready (or when boss is vulnerable), play Knife Trap
5. Melt the enemy

**Counters to Watch:**
- **Knowledge Demon** — Limits cards played per turn. Bad.
- **Entomancer** — Hitting with Shivs = deck full of Daze. Very bad.
- **Strength-stealing enemies** — Shivs have low base damage. Strength loss = 0 damage.
- **Intangible enemies** — Wait until Intangible wears off for Knife Trap.

**Pro Tips:**
- Hidden Daggers discards statuses enemies inflict
- Phantom Blades lets you Retain Shivs for bigger turn
- Up My Sleeve becomes 0-cost in long fights = infinite Shivs
- Goopy Defend enchantment thins deck, helps draw generators faster

## Archetype 4: Block/Weak/Control

**A support package that works with any archetype.**

### Key Block/Control Cards

| Card | Tier | Effect |
|------|------|--------|
| **Piercing Wail** | A | ALL enemies lose 6/8 Strength. Exhausts. COMMON for some reason. |
| **Leg Sweep** | A | Apply 2/3 Weak. Gain 11/14 Block. Best Weak source. |
| **Footwork** | A | Power: Gain 2/3 Dexterity. One copy is enough. |
| **After Image** | A | Power: Gain 1/2 Block per card played. Great with Sly spam. |
| **Blur** | B | Block carries to next turn. Less needed in STS2. |
| **Wraith Form** | S | Ancient. Gain 2/3 Intangible. Game-winning. |

### Control Strategy
1. Apply Weak early (Neutralize, Leg Sweep)
2. Piercing Wail on big attack turns
3. Footwork for passive block
4. After Image if playing many cards
5. Wraith Form if offered as Ancient reward

## Act-by-Act Priorities

### Act 1: Survival + Staying Open

**Priority 1: Acrobatics**
- Best common in the class
- Take it on sight, no questions

**Priority 2: Damage Cards**
- Backstab (0-cost innate 11 damage)
- Dagger Throw (damage + discard)
- Dash (block + damage)

**Priority 3: Block/Control**
- Leg Sweep
- Piercing Wail (why is this common?!)

**Don't commit to archetype yet** — Stay open to what the game offers.

### Act 2: Commit to Sly

**Priority 1: Tools of the Trade**
- Engine card — auto-Sly every turn
- Upgrade to 0 cost is huge

**Priority 2: Sly Payoffs**
- Reflex, Untouchable, Flick-Flack
- Take 2-3 total

**Priority 3: Well-Laid Plans**
- Retain for consistency
- Hold Acrobatics for perfect turns

**Start skipping cards** that don't fit Sly/Discard.

### Act 3: Go Infinite

**Focus on:**
- Removing remaining Strikes/Defends
- Getting deck to 10-13 cards
- Finding Calculated Gamble if possible
- Upgrade priorities: Acrobatics, Prepared, Tools of the Trade

**You should be able to:**
- Play entire deck for free most turns
- Kill enemies turn 1-2
- Never take damage

## Relic Priorities

### S-Tier for Silent
- **Kunai** — +1 Dex per 3 attacks. Shiv synergy if forced.
- **Ornamental Fan** — 4 block per 3 attacks.
- **Letter Opener** — 5 damage per 3 Skills. Skill-heavy deck.
- **Snecko Skull** — +1 Poison applied. If doing poison.

### Good Synergies

- Block relics (supplement Sly block)
- Exhaust payoffs (many cards exhaust)

### Avoid
- **Velvet Choker** — 6 card limit kills card spam
- **Runic Pyramid** — Don't need to keep hand, want to discard!

## Common Mistakes

1. **Going Shivs** — The archetype is dead. Blade Dance exhausts now.
2. **Going Poison** — No Catalyst. Much weaker than STS1.
3. **Not taking Acrobatics** — BEST CARD IN CLASS. Always take it.
4. **Ignoring Tools of the Trade** — Auto-Sly every turn. Click it.
5. **Too many Sly cards** — Need discard enablers too. Balance.
6. **Not removing Strikes/Defends** — Small deck = more Sly triggers.
7. **Backflip over Acrobatics** — Backflip draws but doesn't discard. Bricks.

## STS2 vs STS1 Changes

### Much Worse
- **Blade Dance** — Now EXHAUSTS. Shiv archetype is dead.
- **Catalyst** — REMOVED. Poison doesn't scale anymore.
- **Corpse Explosion** — REMOVED. Can't spread poison.
- **Calculated Gamble** — Now EXHAUSTS permanently.

### Much Better
- **Sly keyword** — Cards play FREE when discarded. Broken.
- **Prepared+** — Draw 2, discard 2. Was terrible, now amazing.
- **AoE matters** — Many multi-enemy fights make Flick-Flack great.

### Same Feel
- Weak is still amazing
- Draw/discard is still the core identity
- Small decks still win

## Quick Reference: Always Pick

**S-Tier (Never Skip):**
- Acrobatics, Prepared, Tools of the Trade, Well-Laid Plans
- Reflex, Calculated Gamble, Adrenaline
- Wraith Form (Ancient), Suppress (Ancient)

**A-Tier (Usually Pick):**
- Pinpoint, Leg Sweep, Piercing Wail, Footwork
- Noxious Fumes, Corrosive Wave (if poison)
- After Image, The Hunt

**Avoid (Even if Offered):**
- Shiv cards (Blade Dance, Finisher, Knife Trap)
- Most poison cards (Deadly Poison, Bouncing Flask, Snakebite)
- Storm of Steel, Shadow Step
