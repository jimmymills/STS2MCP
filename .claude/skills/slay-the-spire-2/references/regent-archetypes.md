## Character Overview

### Why Regent is Top Tier

**From video guide:** "The Regent has access to absurd scaling potential, powerful infinite loops, and the ability to turn huge offense into staggering amounts of defense."

**Two Main Archetypes:**
1. **Star Engine** (S-Tier) — Generate stars, convert to damage/defense
2. **Sovereign Blade** (B-Tier) — Forge a big sword, swing once for massive damage

### Stars vs Forge Comparison (from video)

**Star Builds - Pros:**
- Better scaling for higher ascensions
- More consistent
- Better flexibility
- Stronger defense
- Excellent combo potential
- Better AoE damage

**Star Builds - Cons:**
- Very draw order reliant
- Easy to overdraft
- Several bait cards

**Forge Builds - Pros:**
- Huge single-target damage
- Very satisfying payoff
- Good for boss killing

**Forge Builds - Cons:**
- Less consistent
- Weaker defense
- Struggles with AoE

**Verdict from video:** "From my testing on higher ascensions, it really comes down to the Star Engine reigning supreme as the best possible consistent archetype."

### Unique Mechanics

**Stars:**
- Second resource alongside Energy
- Generated by cards (Glow, Venerate, Hidden Cache)
- **Stars carry over between turns** (unlike Energy!)
- Many powerful cards cost Stars instead of Energy
- Start combat with 3 Stars (from Crown of Stars relic)

**From video:** "Stars are the extra resource pool. A lot of Regent's best cards cost Stars instead of or in addition to normal energy. The important difference is Stars carry over between turns."

**Forge:**
- Adds damage to your **Sovereign Blade**
- Sovereign Blade = 2 energy attack with Retain
- Forging accumulates throughout combat
- Blade starts at 10 damage

### Starter Deck (10 cards)
```
Strike x4
Defend x4
Venerate x1 (1 energy, gain 2 Stars)
Following Star x1 (0 energy, 2 Stars, deal 7, apply Weak)
```
- Venerate is worse star generation (better options exist)
- Following Star is actually good (0 energy damage + Weak)

### Starting Relic
**Crown of Stars** — Gain 3 Stars at start of combat

## Dominant Strategy: Star Engine + Infinite

**From video:** "Regent is consistently trying to do two things: Convert energy into stars, or convert stars into something that wins fights — damage, block, draw, debuff, or even more energy."

### Why Stars Are Broken
1. Stars carry over between turns (unlike Energy)
2. Star-cost cards are often 0 Energy
3. Convergence retains your hand = Runic Pyramid effect
4. Glow is a common that makes stars AND draws
5. Small deck + Glow + Convergence = infinite

### Star Conversion Loop

**From video:** "Once you have Stars, you don't just sit on them. You convert them back into Energy to keep your turn going indefinitely."

**Conversion Cards:**
| Card | Effect | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| **Alignment** | 0 energy, 2 Stars → 2 Energy | Key loop piece |
| **Big Bang** | 0 energy → draw 1, +1 energy, +1 star | Self-sustaining |
| **Convergence** | 1 energy → +1 energy/star next turn, RETAIN hand | Infinite enabler |

**The Loop:**
1. Generate stars (Glow, Hidden Cache)
2. Convert stars to energy (Alignment)
3. Use energy to draw (Glow draws 2)
4. Play payoffs
5. Convergence to retain hand
6. Repeat

## Archetype 2: Sovereign Blade (B-Tier)

**THIS ARCHETYPE IS WEAKER. Don't build around it unless forced.**

### What Is Forge?

**From video:** "Forge builds up the Sovereign Blade, Regent's signature sword. Whenever you forge, you either create the blade if you don't have it, or buff it if you already do."

- Cards say "Forge X" = add X damage to Sovereign Blade
- Sovereign Blade = 2 energy, Retain, deals accumulated damage
- Blade starts at 10 damage

### Why It's Weaker

**From video:** "Waiting for the Sovereign Blade to come around leaves you extremely dependent on building more of a hybrid with stronger defense."

1. Sovereign Blade costs 2 energy (too expensive)
2. You need multiple Forge cards + draw the Blade
3. Star package does the same thing faster
4. Forge cards don't do enough on their own
5. Downtime while waiting for Blade

### The Good Forge Cards (If You Must)

| Card | Effect | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| **Big Bang** | Draw + energy + star + Forge 5 | S-tier but not FOR Forge |
| **Bulwark** | 13 Block + Forge 10 | Best defensive Forge card |
| **Wrought in War** | Damage + Forge 5 | Strike replacement |
| **Summon Forth** | Get Blade from anywhere | Consistency piece |
| **Conqueror** | Sovereign Blade deals DOUBLE damage | "One of the best really." |
| **The Smith** | Forge 40 | Big number if committed |
| **Seeking Edge** | Sovereign Blade hits ALL enemies | AoE finisher |

### Forge Combo (If Committed)

**From video:** "If you get a few combinations together with something like The Smith that can forge 40, you can have your sovereign blade throw down the hammer, clone it with Heirloom Hammer, forge both, then Seeking Edge to hit all enemies. Absolute destruction."

**Setup:**
1. Summon Forth to get Blade
2. Forge cards to buff it
3. Conqueror to double damage
4. Seeking Edge for AoE
5. Swing for 100+ to all enemies

**Problem:** This is hard to assemble. Stars infinite is easier.

## Archetype 3: Bombardment (A-Tier if Found Early)

**A defensive strategy where Bombardment does all the work.**

### How It Works
1. Get Bombardment early (rare — may not see it)
2. Block every turn
3. Bombardment deals 18-24 damage every turn
4. Enemy dies while you don't take damage

### Key Cards
| Card | Effect | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| **Bombardment** | 3 energy, Power: deal 18/turn at end of turn | One card boss solve. Pick early = S tier. |
| **Particle Wall** | 0 energy, 2 Stars, 9 block repeatable | Premium block |
| **Child of the Stars** | Power: gain block = stars spent | Mental Fortress for stars |
| **Glitterstream** | 1 energy, 11 block next turn + 4 now | Good block common |

### Strategy
1. Find Bombardment early
2. Pick every block card
3. Get Child of the Stars if possible
4. Block while Bombardment kills
5. Easy wins

**Problem:** Bombardment is rare. Can't rely on seeing it.

## Archetype 4: Colorless/Card Creation (B-Tier)

**Viable if you get Supermassive early. Not a primary strategy.**

### How It Works
The key is that **transforming cards counts as creating them**. STS2 throws tons of Status cards at you — this build weaponizes them.

1. Get Supermassive in Act 1
2. Use GUARDS!!!/BEGONE!!!/CHARGE!! to transform Status cards
3. Supermassive scales with each transform
4. Pillar of Creation gives block per transform

### Key Cards
| Card | Effect | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| **Supermassive** | Deal 5 + 3 per card created | Core of the build. Pick early. |
| **GUARDS!!!** | Transform any cards in hand → Minion Sacrifice | Instant block + Supermassive fuel |
| **BEGONE!!!** | Deal 4, transform card → Minion Dive Bomb | Exhaust + damage + fuel |
| **CHARGE!!!** | Exhaust 2 from draw → Minion Strikes | Targeted exhaust + fuel |
| **Crash Landing** | 21 AoE, fill hand with Debris | Debris = colorless = fuel |
| **Pillar of Creation** | Power: 3 block per card created | Defensive payoff |
| **Quasar** | 0 cost, choose 1 of 3 colorless to add | Discovery + fuel |
| **Arsenal** | Power: colorless → 1 Strength | Strength scaling from colorless |

### Why It Works (from IGN)
"The advantage to a transformation-focused Colorless build is that all those useless Slimed, Dazed, Infected, etc. cards are now a genuine asset."

### Strategy
1. Find Supermassive early in Act 1
2. Pick every transform card (GUARDS, BEGONE, CHARGE)
3. Let enemies give you Status cards
4. Transform Status → Minions
5. Supermassive scales to 30+ damage

### When to Build This
- Supermassive in first few floors
- Lots of Status-inflicting enemies (common in STS2)
- Can't find Glow/Convergence

### Don't Build This If
- No early Supermassive
- Already have Star engine pieces
- Blade package started

## Act-by-Act Priorities

### Act 1: Hardest Act

**From written guide:** "Your biggest threat in Act 1 is your own greed. Don't draft slow, expensive scaling cards early. If your deck cannot consistently generate Block and deal raw damage by turn two, the first Elite will crush you."

**Priority 1: Glow**
- Best common in the class
- Stars + draw = enables everything
- Take every copy you see

**Priority 2: Early Damage**
- Knockout Blow (30 damage + stars)
- Guiding Star (10 damage + draw next turn)
- Following Star already in deck

**Priority 3: Block**
- Glitterstream
- Gather Light
- Particle Wall if you have star gen

**Avoid:**
- Forge cards
- Expensive star payoffs without generators
- Card creation package

### Act 2: Come Online

**Priority 1: Convergence**
- THE infinite enabler
- Retain hand = line up combos every turn
- Take it immediately

**Priority 2: Star Payoffs**
- Comet (if you have star gen)
- Gamma Blast
- Reflect

**Priority 3: Child of the Stars**
- Best power in the class
- Block = stars spent
- Never take damage again

**Start skipping cards that don't fit the Stars package.**

### Act 3: Victory Lap

**If built correctly:**
- Loop Glow for stars
- Convergence to retain
- Play Comet for 33/44/55/66+ damage
- Particle Wall for block
- Easy wins

**Focus on:**
- Removing remaining Strikes/Defends
- Finding upgrades for Glow (3 stars)
- Boss relics that help (Throne = gain star per card played)

## Upgrade Priority

| Priority | Cards |

| **Medium** | Particle Wall (12 block), Foregone Conclusion (seek 3) |

## Relic Priorities

### S-Tier for Regent
- **Ice Cream** — Energy carries over = even more resources
- **Runic Pyramid** — Keep hand (redundant with Convergence)
- **Throne (Boss)** — Gain star per card played. Infinite fuel.

### Good Synergies
- Energy relics (still useful)
- Draw relics (find Glow/Convergence faster)
- Block relics (supplement Particle Wall)

### Avoid
- **Velvet Choker** — 6 card limit kills infinite loops
- Relics that need attacks (you loop skills mostly)

## Common Mistakes

1. **Building Forge** — The blade package is a trap. Stars are better.
2. **Taking expensive payoffs without generators** — Comet without Hidden Cache = unplayable
3. **Ignoring Glow** — Best common. Take every copy.
4. **Not getting Convergence** — THE infinite enabler. Click it.
5. **Too many star generators** — Need balance of generators and payoffs
6. **Bloating deck** — Small decks go infinite faster
7. **Overdrafting** — "Very common mistake many of us make is trying to include too many cards."

## Pick a Lane (from written guide)

"The most important advice I can give you is to pick a lane. If you try to build a deck that generates Stars while simultaneously trying to Forge the Sovereign Blade, you will end up with a bloated mess that fails at both. Commit to your win condition early, keep your deck clean."
