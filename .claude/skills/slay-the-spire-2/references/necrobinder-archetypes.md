## Character Overview

### Why Necrobinder is Unique

You're not just managing your own health pool — you're managing a **dual unit setup** featuring a massive reanimated hand named **Osty**.

**Key characteristics from video guide:**
- Low base HP pool (66 HP) compensated by Osty as HP shield
- Osty's HP is block that **doesn't expire between turns** and has **no limits**
- Very synergistic combo potential in native starter cards alone
- One of the most **strength-independent** characters — base damage less important than modificators

### Unique Mechanics

**Osty (The Skeletal Hand):**
- Summoned companion that tanks damage for you
- **Summon X** adds HP to Osty (or revives him with X HP if dead)
- Osty's HP carries over between turns (built-in Barricade!)
- Gains 1 HP passively at end of each turn if alive
- Your starter card **Unleash** deals damage equal to Osty's HP

**Key Insight:** If you block, you have damage (Osty stays alive = Unleash hits harder) AND you don't take damage. The dominant strategy is often just blocking.

**Souls:**
- Zero-cost cards that draw 2 and exhaust
- Created by various cards (Capture Spirit, Dirge, Grave Warden, etc.)
- Enable massive card draw and infinite combos
- Soul+ draws 3 cards
- **From video:** "Soul generation is one of those things I wholeheartedly recommend putting a little bit into any build. 10-15 cards plus steady soul generation = extremely reliable deck."

**Doom:**
- At end of enemy's turn: if Doom ≥ enemy HP, enemy dies instantly
- Does NOT scale like poison (stays static)
- Delayed damage — you still need to block while waiting
- **From video:** "Doom is practically the most cost-efficient killing debuff in game. It doesn't go away between turns. If enemy loses HP same turn Doom stacks applied, they die same turn — faster than poison in optimal circumstances."

**Ethereal:**
- Cards with Ethereal exhaust at end of turn if not played
- Many Necrobinder cards have Ethereal "for no reason" (it's a synergy enabler)
- Triggers Pagestorm, Spirit of Ash, Banshee's Cry discount
- **From video:** "Ethereal is a blessing in disguise — you can decide to let them expire and thin out your deck."

### Starter Deck (10 cards)
```
Strike x4
Defend x4
Unleash x1 (Osty deals damage equal to his HP)
Bodyguard x1 (Summon 5)
```

### Starting Relic
**Osty's Binding** — Summons Osty at start of combat

## Dominant Strategy: Block + Osty

**Why blocking is so strong on Necrobinder:**
1. Osty absorbs damage instead of you
2. Osty's HP = your damage via Unleash
3. Block commons are absurdly overtuned (Defy, Grave Warden, Negative Pulse)
4. Fights naturally go long → block stacking wins
5. Most enemies don't punish sitting around blocking

### Core Block Cards

| Card | Energy | Effect | Why It's Broken |
|------|--------|--------|-----------------|
| **Defy** | 1 | Ethereal. 6 block, 1 Weak | Basically 20 block for 1. Best common. |
| **Grave Warden** | 1 | 8 block, add Soul to draw pile | Shrug it Off + draw 2 later. **From video:** "Very potent. May get nerfed." |
| **Negative Pulse** | 1 | 5 block, 7 Doom to ALL | Block + AoE damage at common. **From video:** "11 damage AoE monster that keeps you alive — best jack of all trades Doom skill." |
| **Death's Door** | 1 | 6 block, if Doom applied: block 3x | 18 block for 1 with Doom synergy |

**Gameplan:**
1. Pick every block common you see
2. Never take chip damage in hallways
3. Farm fights safely for card rewards
4. Stack Osty HP → Unleash kills things
5. Add one damage scaling solution for bosses

## Primary Archetypes

### 1. Soul Infinite (S-Tier)

**The Problem:** Souls + energy generation = infinite almost every run.

### Two-Card Infinite
**Capture Spirit + Borrowed Time**
1. Play Capture Spirit → add 3 Souls to draw pile
2. Play Borrowed Time → gain 1 energy
3. Play Soul → draw 2, get back both cards + 3 remaining Souls
4. Repeat forever

### Key Cards

**Soul Generators:**
| Card | Tier | Effect |
|------|------|--------|
| **Capture Spirit** | S | 3 Souls for 1 energy + damage |
| **Dirge** | S | X cost, Summon 3X, add X Souls |
| **Grave Warden** | A | 1 Soul + 8/11 block |
| **Glimpse Beyond** | A | 4 Souls for 1 energy |
| **Severance** | B | 3 Souls in different zones |

**Energy Generation:**
| Card | Tier | Effect |
|------|------|--------|
| **Borrowed Time** | A | 0 cost, gain 1-2 energy, self-Doom |
| **Neurosurge** | S | 0 cost, 3 energy + draw 2 |
| **Friendship** | S | +1 energy per turn power |
| **Domain** | S | +1 energy + draw per turn |
| **Invoke** | A | 0 cost, +1 energy next turn |
| **Delay** | A | 0 cost, +2 energy next turn (upgraded) |

**From video:** "Invoke, Delay, and Borrowed Time are really powerful cards that do a lot of heavy lifting. Chaining them with Souls is a very powerful foundation for any build."

**Infinite Payoffs:**
- **Unleash** (starter) — Damage scales with Osty HP
- **Haunt** — 6 damage per Soul played (ignores block!)
- **Hang** — Doubles damage each play
- **Banshee's Cry** — 33 AoE after Ethereal cards
- **Soul Storm** — Damage based on Souls played this combat

### Warning Signs
If you have Soul generation + energy generation, you're probably infinite. Don't need to try hard — it just happens.

### 2. Doom Build (A-Tier)

**From video:** "Doom is another mechanic unique to the Necrobinder. Once the enemy's HP is lower than their doom stacks, they perish at the end of their turn."

### Important Distinction from Poison
- **Poison:** 10 stacks = 10+9+8+7... over many turns
- **Doom:** 10 stacks = instant death when HP ≤ 10
- Doom requires enemies to act once before dying (prepare to block!)
- Doom doesn't decay — stays until execution

### Core Doom Cards

| Card | Energy | Effect | Notes |
|------|--------|--------|-------|
| **Countdown** | 1 | Power: 6 Doom to random enemy each turn | Bridge card — enables all Doom synergies |
| **Deathbringer** | 2 | 21 Doom + 1 Weak to ALL | Big AoE Doom, ends multi-enemy fights |
| **End of Days** | 3 | 29 Doom to ALL, kill if Doom ≥ HP | Instant execution. Kills Decimillipede. |
| **No Escape** | 1 | 10 Doom + 5 per 10 already on enemy | Exponential stacking. Boss killer. |
| **Blight Strike** | 1 | 8 damage + Doom equal to damage | Doom + direct damage |
| **Scourge** | 1 | 13 Doom + draw 1 | Solid applicator |
| **Doom Spike** | 1 | Damage + Doom | Damage while stacking |

### Doom Synergies
| Card | Effect |
|------|--------|
| **Death's Door** | 18 block if Doom applied |
| **Shroud** | Gain block whenever you apply Doom |
| **Time's Up** | Deal damage equal to enemy's Doom |

### Doom Strategy
1. Get **Countdown** early (enables everything)
2. Take Death's Door for massive block
3. Add No Escape for boss scaling
4. Use End of Days for emergency AoE execution
5. Block while waiting for Doom to execute

### 3. Osty Attacks Build (B-Tier)

**Mechanic:** Cards that make Osty attack multiple times per turn.

### Core Cards
| Card | Tier | Effect |
|------|------|--------|
| **Fetch** | A | 0 cost, Osty deals 3, draw 1 (first play) |
| **Flatten** | B | 2 cost (0 if Osty attacked), 12 damage |
| **Poke** | C | 0 cost, Osty deals 6 |
| **Rattle** | C | Hits additional times per Osty attack this turn |
| **Squeeze** | A | 25 damage + 5 per other Osty attack |

### The Combo
1. Play Fetch → free damage + draw
2. Play Poke → more Osty attacks
3. Play Flatten for 0 → free 12 damage
4. Play Rattle → 3+ hits
5. Play Squeeze → massive finisher

### Supporting Cards
- **Calcify** — Osty attacks deal +4 damage
- **Sentry Mode** — Add Sweeping Gaze (10 Osty damage) each turn
- **Protector** (Ancient) — Damage based on Osty's MAX HP

### Assessment
This package is the **weakest** of Necrobinder's archetypes. The bridge cards aren't strong enough, and you need perfect draw order for payoff. Block + Souls is just easier.

### 4. Ethereal Package (A-Tier)

**From video:** "Ethereal cards can be heavily weaponized. They can be reduced out of your deck if not needed."

### Why Ethereal Matters
- **Banshee's Cry** costs 2 less per Ethereal card played (33 AoE for 0!)
- **Spirit of Ash** gives 4 block per Ethereal card played
- **Pagestorm** draws a card when you draw Ethereal cards
- Bad Ethereal cards just exhaust themselves (deck thinning)

### Key Ethereal Cards
| Card | Effect |
|------|--------|
| **Defy** | Best block common |
| **Defile** | 13 damage, Ethereal (Hemokinesis without downside) |
| **Enfeebling Touch** | -8 Strength, Ethereal |
| **Parse** | Draw 3, Ethereal |
| **Domain** | +1 energy/draw per turn, Ethereal |

### Ethereal Payoffs
| Card | Effect |
|------|--------|
| **Banshee's Cry** | 33 AoE, costs 2 less per Ethereal played |
| **Spirit of Ash** | +4 block per Ethereal card played |
| **Pagestorm** | Draw 1 when you draw Ethereal |
| **Pull from Below** | 5 damage per Ethereal played this combat |

### Strategy
1. Pick Ethereal cards naturally (they're all good)
2. If you see Banshee's Cry, commit harder
3. Spirit of Ash makes blocking trivial
4. Pagestorm fills your hand constantly

**From written guide:** "Scythe is a rare attack that deals 13 damage for two energy. Terrible on paper, but every single time you use Scythe, its damage permanently increases for the rest of the run."

## Act-by-Act Priorities

### Act 1: Hardest Part
- Necrobinder struggles early — advanced hallways are scary
- Prioritize block commons (Defy, Grave Warden)
- Don't run into every elite — 1-2 is fine
- Aim for Soul/energy pieces to enable scaling
- One damage card (Defile, Death March) helps close fights

### Act 2: Come Online
- You should have infinite pieces or Doom scaling by now
- Elites are manageable with block stacking
- Focus on thinning deck (remove Strikes/Defends)
- Add consistency (Dredge, Pagestorm)

### Act 3: Victory Lap
- If built correctly, fights are trivial
- Infinite loops kill everything
- Block spam means you never take damage
- Main challenge is not getting bored

## Relic Priorities

### S-Tier for Necrobinder
- **Charon's Ashes** — 3 AoE damage per exhaust (Ethereal exhausts!)
- **Ghost Seed** — Strikes/Defends exhaust end of turn
- Any energy relic

### Good Synergies
- Exhaust relics (so many Ethereal cards)
- Draw relics (Soul generation already draws tons)

### Avoid
- **Velvet Choker** — 6 card limit kills infinite

## Common Mistakes

1. **Mixing archetypes badly** — Osty attacks + Doom + Souls all together = unfocused
2. **Not blocking enough** — Your damage comes from blocking (Osty HP)
3. **Ignoring Ethereal value** — Even without synergies, Ethereal thins your deck
4. **Fighting too many elites Act 1** — Necrobinder is fragile early
5. **Overthinking** — Just pick block cards and Soul generators, you'll win
6. **Not using Retain** — Snap is incredible for lining up combos

## The Infinite Problem

**Developer note:** Most Necrobinder runs end up infinite because:
- Souls are too efficient (0 cost draw 2)
- Energy generation is abundant (Borrowed Time, Neurosurge, Friendship)
- Exhaust is everywhere (targeted + Ethereal)
- Small decks are easy to achieve

**Expected nerfs:**
- Soul draws reduced
- Energy generation costs increased
- Exhaust made harder

Until then, enjoy the free wins but know it's not intended balance.
