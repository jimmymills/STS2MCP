# Slay the Spire 2 - Ascension Levels

## Overview

Ascension is the difficulty system in Slay the Spire 2. Unlike the original game's 20 levels, STS2 currently has **10 Ascension levels** (early access). However, this doesn't mean it's easier — Mega Crit compressed multiple modifiers into single levels, making the difficulty curve extremely steep.

**Key Points:**
- Unlock Ascension by beating Act 3 for the first time
- Each level adds a new modifier that **stacks** with all previous modifiers
- Progress is **character-specific** in solo play
- Co-op has separate progression and universal unlocks

---

## Ascension Levels & Modifiers

| Level | Modifier Name | Effect |
|-------|---------------|--------|
| **A1** | Swarming Elites | Elite enemies spawn more often across all three Acts |
| **A2** | Weary Traveler | Ancients only heal 80% of your missing HP (was 100%) |
| **A3** | Poverty | Enemies and treasure chests drop 25% less gold |
| **A4** | Tight Belt | Start each run with 1 less potion slot (2 instead of 3) |
| **A5** | Ascender's Bane | Start each run with Ascender's Bane curse (Unplayable, Ethereal, Eternal — cannot be removed!) |
| **A6** | Gloom | Each Act has fewer Rest Sites on the map |
| **A7** | Scarcity | Rare and Upgraded cards appear less frequently |
| **A8** | Tough Enemies | All enemies have more HP |
| **A9** | Deadly Enemies | All enemies deal more damage |
| **A10** | Double Boss | Fight TWO bosses at the end of Act 3 |

---

## Modifier Details

### A1: Swarming Elites
- More Elite nodes appear on maps
- Forces more risk/reward decisions
- **Strategy:** Hunt Elites aggressively in Act 1 for relics; treat HP as currency

### A2: Weary Traveler
- Ancient Boons heal 80% of missing HP instead of 100%
- Less recovery between acts
- **Strategy:** Value sustain relics and Rest Sites more

### A3: Poverty
- 25% less gold from all sources
- Harder to afford shop cards, relics, and removes
- **Strategy:** Prioritize gold-efficient decisions; skip unnecessary purchases

### A4: Tight Belt
- Start with 2 potion slots instead of 3
- Less safety net for bad draws
- **Strategy:** Use potions aggressively to secure Elite kills, don't hoard

### A5: Ascender's Bane
- Permanent curse in starting deck
- **Unplayable:** Costs 1 energy, does nothing
- **Ethereal:** Exhausts at end of turn if not played (but you can't play it)
- **Eternal:** Cannot be removed by any means
- **Strategy:** Deck thinning becomes mandatory; remove Strikes/Defends to minimize curse draw chance

### A6: Gloom
- Fewer Rest Sites per Act
- Less opportunity to heal or upgrade
- **Strategy:** Plan paths carefully; consider Dig at rest sites for relics

### A7: Scarcity
- Rare cards appear less often in rewards
- Upgraded cards appear less often
- **Strategy:** Value rare cards more when offered; upgrade aggressively at rest sites

### A8: Tough Enemies
- All enemies have increased HP
- Fights take longer
- **Strategy:** Need better damage scaling; can't rely on mediocre damage output

### A9: Deadly Enemies
- All enemies deal more damage
- More punishing when you fail to block
- **Strategy:** Block more carefully; can't tank through fights as easily

### A10: Double Boss
- Act 3 ends with TWO boss fights back-to-back
- Must have enough sustain and deck consistency for both
- **Strategy:** Need a complete, synergistic deck; can't rely on burst alone

---

## Solo vs Co-Op Progression

### Solo Play
- Ascension levels are **character-specific**
- Beating A3 with Ironclad only unlocks A4 for Ironclad
- Other characters stay at their current level
- Must grind each character separately

### Co-Op Play
- Separate save file from solo
- **Universal unlocks:** Beating A7 with any character unlocks A8 for ALL characters
- **Lowest common denominator:** Party plays at the lowest player's max level
- Progress still requires beating the Act 3 boss

### Custom Mode
- Currently (early access) allows Ascension progression
- May be patched out later

## Strategic Tips by Ascension Tier

### Low Ascension (A1-A3)
- Learn to hunt Elites for relics
- Start managing gold more carefully
- Build fundamentally sound decks

### Mid Ascension (A4-A6)
- Use potions liberally, not just for bosses
- Deck thinning becomes critical (Ascender's Bane)
- Plan paths around limited Rest Sites

### High Ascension (A7-A10)
- Cannot force favorite archetypes — adapt to what you're offered
- Rare cards are precious; take good ones when offered
- Need complete, efficient decks to handle double boss
- Every card in your deck must have a purpose
- Deck size of ~20 cards often outperforms 35+ card decks

## Comparison to Slay the Spire 1

| STS1 | STS2 |
|------|------|
| 20 Ascension levels | 10 Ascension levels |
| Modifiers spread thin | Modifiers compressed, steeper curve |
| Some easy early levels | Difficulty ramps quickly |
| Final: A20 Heart fight | Final: A10 Double Boss |

Many STS1 modifiers were combined into single STS2 levels, making each rank-up more impactful.

**At A5:** Swarming Elites + 80% Ancient heal + 25% less gold + 1 less potion slot + Ascender's Bane

**At A10:** All of the above PLUS fewer Rest Sites + rarer cards + enemies have more HP + enemies deal more damage + TWO Act 3 bosses
