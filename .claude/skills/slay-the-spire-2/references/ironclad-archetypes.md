# Ironclad Archetypes - Slay the Spire 2

## Meta Overview

In STS2, Ironclad is dominated by **Infinite** strategies. The player can force infinite combos almost every run due to:
- Bloodletting at Common rarity (reusable energy)
- Multiple uncommon energy generators (Expect to Fight, Forgotten Ritual)
- Strong draw/exhaust synergies at common/uncommon

**Key insight:** Act 1 is the only real challenge. After surviving Act 1, execute your infinite loop.

**Starting Relic - Burning Blood:** Heals 6 HP after every combat. This passive sustain means you can make aggressive plays in Act 1 and take chip damage to push damage output. Don't overblock — sometimes taking 5 damage to deal 20 is correct.

## Primary Archetypes

### 1. Exhaust Infinite (S-Tier Strategy)

**Summary:** The dominant Ironclad strategy. Exhaust down to a tiny deck loop that generates more energy/draw than it costs, then play infinite attacks.

📺 **Video Reference:** [How To Win With the Ironclad](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=b_K0WRmNgwY) - Comprehensive guide covering why exhaust is so powerful and how to build infinites.

📺 **Video Reference:** [Ironclad 2 Card Infinite Combo](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zSkM_GSBweI) - Demonstrates the Pommel Strike + Hellraiser infinite that plays itself.

**The Hellraiser Infinite (Easiest Method):**
- 2x Pommel Strike + Hellraiser
- Hellraiser triggers Pommel Strikes when drawn
- Thin deck to ~10 cards or less
- Loop cycles automatically until enemy dies

**Core Exhaust Cards:**
| Card | Role | Priority |
|------|------|----------|
| Bloodletting | Energy (reusable, Common!) | **S-Tier** |
| Burning Pact | Exhaust + Draw 2 | **S-Tier** |
| Pommel Strike | Damage + Draw (Draw 2 upgraded) | **S-Tier** |
| Spite | 0-cost attack + Draw on self-damage | **S-Tier** |
| Stoke | Mass exhaust + random cards | **A-Tier** |
| Second Wind | Mass exhaust (Skills) | **A-Tier** |
| True Grit+ | Targeted exhaust | **A-Tier** |
| Fiend Fire | Exhaust entire hand for damage | **B-Tier** |

**Support Powers:**
| Card | Effect |
|------|--------|
| Dark Embrace | Draw 1 when card exhausts (BROKEN) |
| Feel No Pain | Gain 3-4 Block when card exhausts |
| Forgotten Ritual | Gain energy when card exhausts |
| Expect to Fight | Gain energy from attacks in hand |

**The Holy Trinity:** Corruption + Dark Embrace + Feel No Pain
- Corruption: All Skills cost 0, exhaust when played
- Dark Embrace: Draw on every exhaust
- Feel No Pain: Block on every exhaust
- Result: Play your entire deck in one turn

📺 **Video Reference:** [Going Infinite in STS2](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4UduYOVbrVU) - Covers infinite combos for all characters, including Ironclad methods with colorless cards.

**Colorless Infinite (Works on any character):**
- Thin deck to ~10 cards
- Finesse (0-cost, draw 1) + Flash of Steel (0-cost, 6 damage, draw 1)
- They keep drawing each other
- Use exhaust cards to get deck small enough

**How to Execute:**
1. **Act 1:** Take exhaust cards + early damage to survive
2. **Mid-run:** Prioritize draw engines (Dark Embrace, Pommel Strike+)
3. **Act 2-3:** Thin aggressively, skip cards that don't serve the infinite
4. **Boss fights:** Set up by exhausting non-loop cards, then loop until dead

**Priority Picks:** Bloodletting > Dark Embrace > Burning Pact > Pommel Strike > Spite > Second Wind

### 2. Vulnerable Synergy / Vicious Build (A-Tier)

**Summary:** Leverage Vulnerable for insane card draw, then transition into damage. Often combines naturally with Exhaust Infinite.

📺 **Video Reference:** [This New Ironclad Card Is RIDICULOUS](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yQtprSu-L58) - Gameplay showcasing Stoke and exhaust synergies.

**The Vicious Engine (Currently Broken):**
- Vicious: Whenever you apply Vulnerable, draw 1 card (draw 2 upgraded!)
- Thunderclap vs 3 enemies = draw 3-6 cards!
- Taunt becomes "gain 7 block, draw 1-2 cards"
- Each vuln application fuels your draw engine

**Core Cards:**
| Card | Role |
|------|------|
| Vicious | Draw on vuln application (BROKEN) |
| Bash | Starter, applies vuln |
| Thunderclap | AoE vuln = massive draw in multi-fights |
| Taunt | Block + vuln |

**Payoff Cards:**
| Card | Effect |
|------|--------|
| Dismantle | Double damage vs vulnerable |
| Bully | Bonus damage per vuln stack |
| Cruelty | +25/50% damage to vuln enemies |

**Why It's Strong:**
- Vicious turns every vuln application into card advantage
- Natural synergy with exhaust (draw through deck faster)
- Thunderclap in multi-enemy fights draws your whole deck
- Usually evolves into Exhaust Infinite as secondary win condition

**Note:** Likely to be nerfed. Vicious+ drawing 2 cards per vuln is absurd.

### 3. Self-Damage / Bloodletting Build (A-Tier)

**Summary:** Turn HP loss into massive benefits. High-risk, high-reward archetype that weaponizes Ironclad's large HP pool.

**Why It Works:**
- Cards that damage you are mathematically stronger per energy
- Ironclad has 80 HP (highest in game) + Burning Blood healing
- Multiple payoffs convert HP loss into Strength, damage, or block

**Core Engine Cards:**
| Card | Effect | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| Rupture | Gain 1 Str when losing HP on your turn | KEY CARD - enables the archetype |
| Bloodletting | Lose 3 HP, gain 2 energy | Common rarity! Also enables infinites |
| Offering | Lose 6 HP, gain 2 energy + draw 3 | Exhaust, but Soul Power enchant removes that |
| Brutality | At turn start: lose 1 HP, draw 1 | Passive Rupture trigger each turn |
| Crimson Mantle | At turn start: lose 1 HP, gain 8 Block | Triggers Rupture, provides defense |

**Damage Payoffs:**
| Card | Effect |
|------|--------|
| Tear Asunder | Deal 5 damage. Hits additional time for each HP lost this combat |
| Inferno | Whenever you lose HP, deal AoE damage |
| Spite | Draw 1 when losing HP |
| Hemokinesis | Lose 2 HP, deal 15 damage |

**Essential Cards:**
| Card | Why |
|------|-----|
| Reaper | Heals you for unblocked damage dealt. MANDATORY late-game recovery |
| Feed | +3 Max HP on kill. Expands your damage pool |
| Limit Break | Double your Strength (enables explosive finishes) |

**Build Requirements:**
- Must find Rupture (otherwise self-damage is just losing)
- Must find Reaper (otherwise you die before winning)
- Feed helps but isn't mandatory

**Relics:**
- **Centennial Puzzle:** Draw 3 cards first time you lose HP each combat
- **Runic Cube:** Draw 1 when losing HP
- **Beating Remnant:** Can't lose HP below 20 (safety net)

**How to Play:**
1. Find Rupture early — without it, skip this archetype
2. Take self-damage cards for efficient energy/damage
3. Stack Strength through Rupture triggers
4. Use Tear Asunder or big attacks as finishers
5. Reaper to heal back after explosive damage turn

**Warning:** Don't take this archetype if you can't find Rupture. Self-damage without payoff just kills you.

### 4. Block / Barricade Build (B-Tier)

**Summary:** Stack block that persists, use Body Slam for damage. Fun and unkillable when it works, but requires specific rares.

📺 **Video Reference:** [UNKILLABLE Ironclad Body Slam Tank Build](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GOQO1Hxp15w) - Full run demonstrating the block stacking strategy.

**The Core Combo:**
- **Barricade:** Block doesn't expire at turn start
- **Body Slam:** Deal damage equal to your current Block
- Stack 150+ Block → Body Slam for 150+ damage at 1 energy

**Core Cards:**
| Card | Effect |
|------|--------|
| Barricade | Block persists (REQUIRED) |
| Body Slam | Damage = Block |
| Impervious | Gain 30 Block. Exhaust |
| Entrench | Double your Block |
| Metallicize | Gain 3-4 Block at end of turn |

**Support Cards:**
| Card | Role |
|------|------|
| Flame Barrier | Block + thorns |
| Shrug It Off | Block + draw |
| Iron Wave | Attack + block |
| Juggernaut | Deal 5 damage whenever you gain Block |

**Key Upgrades:**
- Body Slam+ → Costs 0 energy (critical!)
- Limit Break+ → No exhaust (double Strength repeatedly)
- Entrench+ → Costs 1 energy

**How to Play:**
1. Must find Barricade — skip this archetype without it
2. Prioritize block generation cards
3. Play Barricade early in boss fights
4. Stack block over several turns
5. Body Slam for massive burst when safe

**Why It's B-Tier:**
- Requires rare Barricade to function
- Power Through moved to Defect (huge loss)
- Infinite strategies win before block matters
- No heart fight requiring massive block (yet)

**When to Go Block:**
- Early Barricade drop
- Good block synergy relics (Anchor, Orichalcum, Calipers)
- Failed to find infinite pieces

### 5. Strength Scaling Build (C-Tier in STS2)

**Summary:** Stack strength, play multi-hit attacks for massive damage. Classic STS1 strategy, but outclassed by infinites in STS2.

**Why It's Weaker Now:**
- Infinite strategies don't need strength (infinite 6-damage attacks > one big hit)
- Strength cards are dead draws when going infinite
- Self-damage build does strength better with Rupture

**Core Cards:**
| Card | Effect |
|------|--------|
| Demon Form | Gain 2 Strength at start of turn |
| Inflame | Gain 2 Strength |
| Spot Weakness | Gain Strength if enemy attacking |
| Limit Break | Double your Strength |
| Rupture | Strength from HP loss |

**Finisher Cards:**
| Card | Effect |
|------|--------|
| Heavy Blade | 3x Strength scaling |
| Whirlwind | X-cost multi-hit AoE |
| Sword Boomerang | Multi-hit single target |
| Twin Strike | Hit twice |

**When to Use:**
- Can't find infinite pieces
- Early Demon Form + multi-hit attacks
- Desperate Act 1 survival

**Note:** If you're building strength, consider pivoting to Self-Damage build with Rupture instead. You'll get Strength anyway plus better energy economy.

## Quick Reference: What To Pick

**Always Take:**
- Bloodletting, Dark Embrace, Pommel Strike, Burning Pact
- Vicious (especially if upgraded)
- Feed (early game)

**Usually Take:**
- Second Wind, True Grit+, Spite
- Offering (especially with Soul Power enchant)
- Rupture (enables self-damage archetype)

**Situational:**
- Barricade (only if going block build)
- Demon Form (slow, only if no other scaling)
- Heavy attacks (Act 1 survival only)

**Usually Skip:**
- Clash (too conditional)
- Anger (clogs deck with copies)
- Most strength-only cards without Rupture
