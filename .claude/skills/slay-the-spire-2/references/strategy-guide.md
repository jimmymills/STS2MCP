# Slay the Spire 2 - General Strategy Guide

> Core strategies, tips, and mechanics to improve your win rate.

---

## The RNG Illusion

**This game feels RNG-based, but it isn't.**

When you die with no block cards against a big attack, it feels like bad luck. But top players in Slay the Spire 1 achieved 70+ win streaks on Ascension 20 Heart kills — the hardest content in the franchise. If it were truly RNG, this would be impossible.

**The truth:** Every loss can be traced back to decisions YOU made:
- Card picks that didn't synergize
- Skipping card removal opportunities  
- Poor pathing choices
- Not using potions when you should have
- Taking damage you didn't need to take

**Why this matters:** If you blame RNG, you can't improve. Accept that losses are learning opportunities, and you'll get better fast.

> 💡 **Pro tip:** After every death, spend 2 minutes asking "Why did I actually die?" Look at your deck — was it actually good? What cards didn't fit? This "run autopsy" is the fastest way to improve.

## Character Overview

### Ironclad (Warrior)
- **Focus:** Strength scaling, block synergies
- **Key Mechanics:**
  - Strength boosts (especially potent with multi-hit attacks)
  - Block synergies (Juggernaut deals damage from blocking, Body Slam weaponizes block)
  - Strike archetype (Perfected Strike, Hellraiser plays Strikes for free)

### Silent (Rogue/Assassin)
- **Focus:** Poison, Shivs, Discard synergies
- **Key Mechanics:**
  - **Poison:** Damage over time that ticks every turn (Accelerant triggers poison multiple times)
  - **Shivs:** Zero-cost 4-damage attacks, single use
  - **Sly:** When discarded, the card is played instead — enables hand-dump combos
  - Lots of cycle/discard effects

### Regent (New Character)
- **Focus:** Dual resource system (Energy + Stars)
- **Key Mechanics:**
  - **Stars:** Blue resource generated by cards, used as alternate costs
  - Cards with star costs have lower energy costs
  - Forged Blade follows you between fights
  - Requires resource management — mismanagement = fewer plays per turn

### Necrobinder (New Character)
- **Focus:** Summons, Doom, Souls
- **Key Mechanics:**
  - **Austy:** Summoned companion that takes damage for you (deceptively tanky)
  - **Doom:** Instantly kills enemies when Doom exceeds their HP at end of turn
  - **Souls:** Stack souls for draw and damage scaling
  - ⚠️ Austy cards don't scale with Necrobinder's stats (buffs/debuffs don't transfer)

### Defect (Robot)
- **Focus:** Orbs, Zero-cost synergies
- **Key Mechanics:**
  - **Orbs:** Have passive effects + active effects when evoked
    - Lightning: Passive damage → Evoke for big damage
    - Frost: Passive block → Evoke for big block
    - Dark: Builds damage → Evoke to unleash all
    - Plasma: Gives energy
  - **Evoke:** Triggers when orb slots fill or card says "evoke"
  - Zero-cost card synergies (Claw builds damage exponentially)

## Diagnosing Where You Die

### Dying in Act 1?
**Causes:**
- Poor pathing (running into elites unprepared)
- Not enough damage cards
- Not hitting rest sites before elites

**Fix:** Focus on damage cards early, path with options, upgrade before first elite

### Dying in Act 2?
**Causes:**
- Taking Act 2 elites too early
- No scaling in your deck
- Deck isn't focused (too many random cards)

**Fix:** Build scaling, commit to an archetype, start skipping cards that don't fit

### Dying in Act 3?
**Causes:**
- Played too cautiously in Acts 1-2
- Not enough relics from elites
- Bad draws still possible (didn't remove Strikes/Defends)

**Fix:** Be greedier early, remove bad cards, plan specifically for the boss

## Map Navigation — The Key to Act 1

> Poor pathing is the #1 reason new players die in Act 1.

### Reading the Map (In Order)
1. **Check the boss** — Know what you're building toward
2. **Count late elites** — Elites after the treasure chest line are easier (you're stronger)
3. **Count total elites** — Aim for 2-3 minimum; 1 elite = failure
4. **Find rest sites** — Want one before first elite to upgrade
5. **Locate shops** — Early shop = damage spike; Late shop = spend accumulated gold

### Elite Pathing Rules
| Elites in Act | Assessment |
|---------------|------------|
| 1 elite | **Failure** — not enough relics to scale |
| 2 elites | Acceptable minimum |
| 3 elites | Good — standard target |
| 4 elites | Excellent — if you can handle it |

### Path with Options (Critical!)
**Good path:** Branches let you choose elite OR rest site when you arrive
- If struggling → take rest site
- If strong → fight elite
- Still have options for later elites

**Bad path:** Single line of combat → combat → elite with no escape
- If you're struggling, you just die
- No way to recover

> 💡 **Example:** If your path goes combat → fire → elite, but you can also branch to combat → fire → shop, you have OPTIONS. Build in fail-safes.

### Fire Between Elites
- Always try to hit a rest site before your first elite
- Upgrading a damage card (12 → 18 damage) makes elites significantly easier
- Fire between elites lets you upgrade OR heal based on need

## Act-by-Act Checkpoints

### Act 1 Checkpoints

#### 1. Take Damage Cards First
- First 3-4 card rewards: prioritize damage over everything
- Cards that deal more than 6 damage (Strike baseline)
- Examples: Defile (13), Headbutt, Perfected Strike, Flatten

#### 2. The 100 Damage Benchmark
Elites have ~140 HP and hit for ~20/turn. You need to kill them in 3-4 turns.

**Target: 100 damage in 3 turns**
- Base deck maximum: 3 Strikes × 6 = 18 damage/turn = 54 total ❌
- With 2-3 damage cards: ~33 damage/turn = 100 total ✅

If you can't hit this benchmark, you're not ready for elites.

#### 3. Elite Priority
- Damage > Block for elites (you can't block 17+ reliably)
- Most elites scale damage every turn — you MUST kill them fast
- All damage, no block is better than all block, no damage

#### 4. Shop Strategy
| Gold | Strategy |
|------|----------|
| < 100 | Skip shop (unless desperate for potion) |
| 100-200 | Card remove, maybe a damage card |
| 200-300 | Strong attack cards, card removes |
| 300+ | Relic (permanent scaling) |

Early shop before elite → buy damage
Late shop after fights → buy relic with accumulated gold

#### 5. Rest Site Rules
- **Above 50% HP:** Upgrade (upgrading damage prevents MORE damage than resting heals)
- **Below 50% HP:** Consider resting
- Upgrade your best damage card before first elite

#### 6. Use Potions Aggressively
- First elite: Use potions freely
- Saving 10-20 HP is worth a potion
- Fire Potion = flat 20 damage = can push you over the kill threshold

---

### Act 2 Checkpoints

#### 1. Build Scaling (Most Important!)
Act 2 elites have ~200 HP and hit for ~30. You need to get STRONGER during the fight.

**What is scaling?**
Cards/effects that make your deck more powerful as the fight goes on:

| Scaling Type | Examples |
|--------------|----------|
| Strength gain | Demon Form, Limit Break, Fight Me |
| Card improvement | Thrash (exhausts attacks to grow), Hidden Gem |
| Passive damage | Haunt, Noxious Fumes, Orbs |
| Double effects | Echo Form (first card plays twice) |
| Deck thinning | Exhaust effects (remove bad cards mid-fight) |
| Cost reduction | Banshee's Cry (6 → 4 → 2 → 0) |

> 💡 **Key insight:** If you don't have scaling, you WILL die in Act 2. This is the hardest thing to learn in Slay the Spire.

#### 2. Build an Archetype Library
Don't force one build every run. Learn multiple win conditions:

**Example (Ironclad):**
- Demon Form scaling
- Bully + Vulnerable stacking
- Body Slam + Block
- Strength + multi-hit

If you only know Demon Form, you'll lose when you don't see it.

#### 3. Start Skipping Cards
- Act 1: Take most cards (everything beats starter deck)
- Act 2: Only take cards that fit your archetype
- Random "good" cards that don't synergize = bloated deck = bad draws

**Unfocused deck:**
Hand of random cards that don't combo → weak turns

**Focused deck:**
Hand of synergistic cards → powerful combos every turn

#### 4. Act 2 Elite Caution
- Act 2 elites are the HARDEST fights in the game
- Your deck is "just becoming capable" — don't overestimate it
- Take them LATE in the act when you're stronger
- Aim for 2 elites (3 if going well)
- Always have an escape path

---

### Act 3 Checkpoints

#### 1. Act 3 Should Be Easy
If you're dying in Act 3, you were too cautious in Acts 1-2.
- Not enough elites = not enough relics
- Not enough scaling = not enough damage

**Mindset shift:** Act 3 boss should be a victory lap, not a challenge.

#### 2. Make Bad Draws Impossible
Acts 1-2: Make good draws possible
Act 3: Make bad draws IMPOSSIBLE

**What's a bad draw?**
- Hand full of Strikes/Defends
- No block when boss attacks big
- No damage when boss is vulnerable

#### 3. Eliminate Variance

**Card Removal:**
- Remove ALL Strikes by Act 3 if possible
- Remove Defends if you have better block
- Every bad card removed = fewer disaster hands

**Potions:**
- Gambler's Brew: Discard bad hand → draw new one
- Bottled Potential: Completely new hand
- Save a potion specifically for boss disaster hands

**Relics:**
- Pocket Watch: Draw extra cards if you play ≤3 (more draws = fewer dead hands)
- Runic Pyramid: Keep your hand forever (save block for attacks, attacks for openings)
- Choices Paradox: Random card with Retain (emergency option)

#### 4. Draw Manipulation
Every character has tools to control draws:
- **Retain:** Save good cards for when you need them
- **Headbutt:** Put a card on top of your deck for next turn
- **Hologram:** Retrieve cards from discard

These cards are MUCH stronger than they look — they eliminate RNG.

#### 5. Plan for the Boss
Before entering boss fight:
- What's my scaling?
- What potions do I have?
- What's my worst possible hand? How do I survive it?

## Combat Strategy

### Understanding Card Types
| Type | Function | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| **Attacks** | Deal damage | Some have secondary effects |
| **Skills** | Usually give block | Can have other effects |
| **Powers** | Persistent effects | Play once, lasts all combat |

### Energy Management
- Most cards cost 1-3 energy
- You start with 3 energy per turn (can be modified)
- Plan your turn to use all energy efficiently
- Zero-cost cards are extremely valuable

### Block Mechanics
- Block prevents damage
- **Block clears at the start of YOUR turn** (not enemy's)
- If you have 10 block and take 8 damage → 2 block remains → cleared next turn
- Some cards give Retain to keep block

### When to Block vs. Attack
**Block when:**
- Enemy is dealing more damage than you can kill them with
- You need to survive to set up
- Enemy has multi-hit attack (block value multiplies)

**Attack when:**
- You can KILL the enemy (check your damage!)
- Enemy is buffing (they won't deal damage this turn)
- You have passive damage (poison, orbs) doing the work

> ⚠️ **Always check if you can kill** — don't waste turns blocking when you could just win

## Reading Enemies

### Threat Prioritization
1. **Immediate threats:** Enemy with big attack + low health (kill now)
2. **Short-term threats:** Enemy buffing itself (deal with before it attacks)
3. **Long-term threats:** 
   - Enemies flooding deck with statuses
   - Enemies with Ritual (gains Strength every turn)

### Attack Patterns
Most enemies have **predictable patterns**. Learn them!

**Example — Nibbler:**
1. Big attack
2. Small attack + block
3. Buff + block
4. Repeat

If you see it buffing → next turn is big attack → save your block card with Retain!

### Scaling Enemies
- Many enemies (especially elites) **escalate over time**
- They gain Strength, apply debuffs, or ramp damage
- **Shorter fights = safer fights**
- Don't play for the long game against scaling enemies

### Counter-Strategies
Some enemies punish specific strategies:
- Limit card plays per turn
- Limit damage dealt/taken
- Apply Weak (reduces your attack damage)
- Apply Frail (reduces your block)
- Use potions to get through hard counters

## Resource Management

### Health is a Resource
- **Spending health is okay** — it gets you ahead
- Early game greed (fighting elites with low HP) = more relics = easier late game
- Don't be afraid to take damage for value
- But know when to be conservative (bad deck, scary elite)

### When to Be Greedy
- ✅ Early game (relics snowball power)
- ✅ You have healing options (rest site, potions)
- ✅ Your deck handles the fight well
- ✅ Full health with a strong deck

### When to Be Conservative
- ❌ Deck isn't in a good spot
- ❌ No healing available
- ❌ Elite counters your strategy
- ❌ Act 2 when deck isn't fully online

### Potion Usage
- **Use potions on elites** — don't save them forever
- Potions expire at end of act (Act boss drops new ones)
- Using a potion early in a fight is often better than "saving" it
- If a potion saves 10-20 HP, USE IT
- Save one potion for boss disaster hands (Gambler's Brew, etc.)

## Rest Sites

### Options
1. **Rest:** Heal ~30% max HP
2. **Smith:** Upgrade one card

### When to Rest
- HP is below 50%
- Upcoming fight is dangerous
- No critical upgrades needed

### When to Smith
- HP is healthy (70%+)
- You have a high-impact upgrade target
- Key card becomes much stronger upgraded
- Powers often have great upgrades

> 💡 **Key insight:** Upgrading a damage card can PREVENT more damage than resting would heal. If upgrading lets you kill an elite 1 turn faster, that's 20+ damage avoided.

### Upgrade Priorities
| Priority | Card Types |
|----------|-----------|
| High | Key powers, scaling cards, draw cards |
| Medium | Good attacks, block cards |
| Low | Situational cards, already-efficient cards |

## Events (? Nodes)

### Generally Positive
- Gold rewards (sometimes at HP cost)
- Free upgrades
- Card removal
- Special shops with cheap relics
- Transform effects (random but removes bad cards)

### Potentially Negative
- Random combat encounters
- Curses
- HP loss without good rewards

### Event Strategy
- Events are more positive than negative on average
- But don't overload on events — you need card rewards too
- Look for upgrades and removals
- Transform Strikes/Defends when possible

## Enchantments & Powers (New in STS2)

### What Are Enchantments?
- Special modifiers added to cards
- Can dramatically change card power
- Some unlock new rest site options

### Example: Clone Enchantment
- Adds "Clone" option at rest sites
- Doubles all cards with Clone tag
- Exponential scaling (especially with Claw!)

### Strategy
- Look for enchantments that synergize with your archetype
- Some enchantments are build-defining
- Rest site cloning can be better than upgrading

## Ascension (Difficulty Scaling)

### What Is Ascension?
- Increased difficulty unlocked by winning runs
- Each level adds a modifier
- Pushes you to play tighter and make harder choices

### Example Modifiers
- Ascension 1: More elites
- Ascension 2: Ancients only heal 80% of missing HP
- Higher levels: Enemies hit harder, more HP, worse events

### Ascension Tips
- Master the base game first
- Learn enemy patterns before pushing difficulty
- Greed becomes riskier but also more necessary
- Tighter deck building becomes mandatory

## The Failed Run Autopsy

After every death, ask yourself:

1. **What killed me?** (The actual attack/turn)
2. **Why couldn't I handle it?** (No block? No damage? Bad draw?)
3. **What decisions led to this?**
   - Did I take cards that didn't fit?
   - Did I skip card removal?
   - Did I fight elites I wasn't ready for?
   - Did I waste potions or save them too long?
   - Did I take unnecessary damage earlier?
4. **What would I do differently?**

**The goal:** Stop blaming RNG. Start finding the decision chain that caused the loss.

> "I died to exact damage" → "I took 3 damage in an earlier fight I didn't need to" → "I could have blocked that hit" → Lesson learned.

This habit is how you go from losing to win-streaking.
